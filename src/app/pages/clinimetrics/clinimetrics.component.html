Clinimetrics

Objective tools to measure and control the condition.
Objective measure.
In medical science it is necessary to measure variables to control the variations of results in order to find a treatment or prevention of disease. We need objective data that are related to the quality of treatment.
Clinimetrics is a methodological discipline with a focus on the quality of measurements in medical research and clinical practice. The aim of Clinimetrics is to foster more rational use of measurements, improve the quality of the clinical work and enhance the quality of patient care
Objective tools to obtain data for indicators, rating scales in areas related to symptoms, physical signs, and other characteristics, used to describe or measure a medical condition.
The foundation of evidence-based practice and thorough research is the use of outcome measures. The validity and reliability, as well as the responsiveness of these measurement tools, is a prerequisite in making meaningful patient-centred clinical inferences.
This collection of evidence about metrics use methods that are valid, reliable and accepted for use in healthcare. For practical reasons the concept of has expanded to cover many clinical issues.
Trichoscopy
Trichoscopy performed with a handheld dermoscope or a videodermoscope became an indispensable tool in differential diagnosis of hair and scalp diseases. This makes it a modern, non-invasive technique, which is well accepted by both, dermatologists and patients.
In all non-cicatricial alopecias presence of empty follicular openings is a common trichoscopy finding.
In alopecia areata black dots and micro-exclamation mark hairs and tapered hairs correlate with disease activity, whereas yellow dots and vellus hairs correlate with disease severity.
Clinimetrics Tools
Dermatology Life Quality Index DLQI
The Dermatology life Quality Index is a ten-question questionnaire used to measure the impact of skin disease on the quality of life of an affected person.
Severity of Alopecia Tool SALT
The Severity of Alopecia Tool (SALT) is widely used to assess the extent of scalp-hair loss in AA.
Percent scalp hair loss. This takes into account the percent of the scalp surface with no hair.
